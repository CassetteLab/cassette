New in 1.8.3

• Redesigned full-screen player — a flowing layout with a larger square (1:1) cover that fills the space, and the cover now flies between the player and the queue as you switch. On macOS, the background colors smoothly cross-fade when the track changes.
• A new confirmation toast, Apple Music style — it shows a cover thumbnail and sits just above the mini player. The "added to playlist" toast is tappable and takes you straight to that playlist.
• Offline playback for downloaded m4a tracks that weren't optimized for streaming — new downloads are fixed automatically, and existing downloads are migrated.

Fixed and improved

• Deleting a playlist now tells you when it fails (instead of silently doing nothing), and a successful delete also removes that playlist's offline download so it doesn't linger.
• Clearing your search history now asks you to confirm first.
• Offline reliability: a flaky or empty server response no longer wipes a downloaded album or playlist, downloaded playlists with missing track info repair themselves, and the full-player cover no longer shows the previous track's artwork when you're offline.
• Playback now resumes correctly when you reopen the app after it was fully closed.
• Better contrast: the now-playing indicator and the macOS player controls stay legible on any cover, and the Home-screen widget keeps its text readable on light covers.
• The Ko-fi support banner in Settings is now smaller and more discreet.

Please focus on:
• Opening, closing, and using the redesigned full player — the square cover, and the cover flying to and from the queue.
• The new toast — add a song to a playlist, then tap the toast to open that playlist.
• Offline playback of downloaded m4a tracks.
• Deleting a playlist: a successful delete also removes its offline copy; a failed delete shows an error and keeps the playlist.
• Clearing search history (the confirmation step).
